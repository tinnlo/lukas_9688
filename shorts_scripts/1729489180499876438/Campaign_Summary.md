## Product Overview

**MINISO MS188** is an open-ear AI translation earbuds with a unique **LCD touchscreen charging case**. Unlike competitors (MS156), this model features:

- **Smart Case**: Full-color LCD display with clock, wallpaper, and touch music controls
- **136 Languages**: Real-time AI translation via app
- **Open-Ear Design**: Ear-hook form factor (no in-ear pressure)
- **60-Hour Battery**: Extended usage for travel/work
- **IPX5 Waterproof**: Sweat-proof for fitness use
- **LED Digital Display**: Battery and status indicators

**Sales Performance**: 12,500 units sold (€300K+ revenue) - Strong market validation

## Script Strategy

### Script 1: Smart Case Reveal (Tech Angle)
**Hook Pattern**: Curiosity Gap + Visual Proof
**Target Audience**: Tech enthusiasts, gadget lovers
**Key Differentiator**: LCD touchscreen case (UNIQUE to MS188)

**Narrative Arc**:
- **0-3s**: Close-up case screen swiping → "Has this case a screen?"
- **3-20s**: Showcase case features (clock, wallpaper, touch control) + open-ear design
- **20-30s**: Reveal translation feature (136 languages + case display transcription)
- **30-35s**: CTA

**Anti-AI Elements**:
- Short micro-lines for natural breath pauses
- Human asides: "But that's just the packaging..."
- Irregular pacing through line breaks (not forced pause cues)

**Expected Performance**: High click-through (novel visual hook), appeals to tech/innovation audience

---

### Script 2: Travel Fail (Problem/Solution Angle)
**Hook Pattern**: Problem/Solution (Recommended by analysis)
**Target Audience**: Travelers, international workers, students
**Key Differentiator**: 136-language translation in real scenario

**Narrative Arc**:
- **0-3s**: POV struggling in Paris → "No idea where hotel is"
- **3-12s**: Language barrier frustration (Google Translate too slow, gestures awkward)
- **12-28s**: Solution: MS188 real-time translation (German → French instantly)
- **28-35s**: Proof: 136 languages, app-compatible, open-ear safety
- **35-38s**: CTA

**Anti-AI Elements**:
- Self-corrections and reactions: "Google Translate? Too slow."
- Concrete scenario stakes (lost in Paris)
- Natural conversational flow

**Expected Performance**: Strong resonance with travel pain point, viral potential during holiday/summer seasons

---

### Script 3: Open-Ear Comfort (Before/After Angle)
**Hook Pattern**: Before/After Comparison
**Target Audience**: Fitness users, office workers, long-wear users
**Key Differentiator**: Open-ear design (no in-ear pressure/pain)

**Narrative Arc**:
- **0-3s**: Pain point hook → "Ears hurt after 30 minutes?"
- **3-10s**: In-ear problems (pressure, sweat buildup)
- **10-25s**: Solution: Open-ear design, 60-hour battery, secure fit
- **25-30s**: Proof: IPX5 waterproof, shake test
- **30-33s**: CTA

**Anti-AI Elements**:
- Human frustration beats: "After 2 hours at office? Unbearable."
- Playful aside: "You can even shower with them."
- Mix of short hits and medium sentences

**Expected Performance**: Appeals to fitness/wellness audience, addresses universal pain point (earbud discomfort)

## Notes for Production

### Visual Requirements
- **Hero Shot**: Close-up of case screen swiping (Script 1)
- **B-Roll**:
  - Water splash on earbuds (IPX5 proof)
  - Violent head shake (secure fit)
  - App interface showing translation in real-time
  - Case screen showing different wallpapers

### Audio Production
- Use **ElevenLabs v3 (alpha)** with provided delivery cues
- Target **30-40 seconds** total duration (max 35s for Script 1, 38s for Script 2, 33s for Script 3)
- German voiceover primary, Chinese optional for bilingual markets

### Compliance
- **Electronics Category**: Avoid medical/health claims
- **Translation Accuracy**: Use "AI translation" not "perfect translation"
- **Battery Life**: State "up to 60 hours" (not guaranteed 60)
- **Waterproof**: Specify "IPX5" rating, not "fully waterproof"
