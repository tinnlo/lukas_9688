## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY)

**DO:**
1.  **Leverage Comments:** Actively look for "Hate comments" or skeptical questions on your ads. Make video replies to them. They often outperform standard ads.
2.  **Use Specific Numbers:** "60 Wattstunden" is much more convincing than "Big battery".
3.  **Admit Limitations:** The creator admitted it *can* die quickly if abused ("continuous blasting"). This "damaging admission" makes the positive claims 10x more credible.

**DON'T:**
1.  **Don't Ignore Negativity:** Don't delete negative comments. Use them as content fuel.
2.  **Don't Over-Polish:** A studio shot explaining battery life would look like a lie. A guy in a messy room holding it looks like the truth.
3.  **Don't Be Defensive:** Be confident/educational, not angry. "I don't know where you shop" is a bit sassy but confident.

**OPPORTUNITY:**
1.  **Visual Torture Test:** Instead of just *saying* he used it all week, show a timelapse or a cut of him cleaning a whole car and the battery still being green.
2.  **Comparison Video:** Actually put it next to a Makita blower and run them both until they die. If it wins or comes close (at a fraction of the price), that's a viral goldmine.
3.  **Stitch/Duet:** Stitch a video of someone complaining about a weak blower and offer this as the solution.
