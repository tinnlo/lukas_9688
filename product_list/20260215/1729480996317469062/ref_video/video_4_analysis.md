## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY)

**DO:**
1.  **Show the Steam:** Ensure the food looks piping hot and fresh; steam implies "freshly made" and increases appetite appeal.
2.  **Sound Design (ASMR):** Amplify the sounds of slurping, opening the lid, or the "clack" of the stainless steel to emphasize quality.
3.  **Localize Scenarios:** Specifically mention "School Lunch" (Schulpause) or "Uni" (University) to target the student demographic more aggressively.

**DON'T:**
1.  **bury the Lead:** Don't wait 10 seconds to show the capacity. Show the "3 packs" fitting in the first 5 seconds if possible.
2.  **Mistake Materials:** Ensure the script says "Edelstahl" (Stainless Steel) clearly. The transcript error "Edelstein" (Gemstone) must be avoided in captions/text to maintain credibility.
3.  **Over-length:** 57s is long for a simple product. Cut the "eating" or "lifestyle" filler to get to the CTA by 40s.

**OPPORTUNITY:**
1.  **"Dorm Room Hack":** Frame it specifically as a "Must-have for students without a kitchen."
2.  **Gift Angle:** "The perfect gift for your boyfriend who can't cook." (Humorous angle).
3.  **Heat Retention Test:** Show a thermometer to prove it keeps noodles hot longer than a normal bowl (functional benefit of the lid/insulation).
