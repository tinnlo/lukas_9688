## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY)

**DO:**
1.  **Emphasize Sound:** Ensure the sound of kibble hitting the floor or the toy rattling is audible; ASMR elements trigger engagement.
2.  **Show the "Before":** The "waiting/impatient" phase (as seen in the hook) is crucial to show desire.
3.  **Localize Dialect:** If targeting specific German regions (e.g., Bavaria), a slight dialect accent in VO could boost trust further.

**DON'T:**
1.  **Over-polish:** Do not use studio lighting. It must look like a real living room to maintain the "viral gadget" feel.
2.  **Delay the Action:** Don't spend 10s talking about features before the dog touches it. The dog must touch it by second 3.
3.  **Use unclear audio:** While "natural" is good, ensure the key keywords ("Einkaufswagen", "Spielzeug") are enunciated clearly (unlike the slight mumbling in this sample).

**OPPORTUNITY:**
1.  **Slow Motion:** Add a slow-mo shot of the treat falling out and the dog catching it to dramatize the "win."
2.  **ASMR Version:** A version with NO music, just the clicking of the toy and the dog's crunching, could capture a different audience segment.
3.  **Multiple Breeds:** Show a cut to a cat or a much smaller dog using it to prove the "versatility" claim visually.
