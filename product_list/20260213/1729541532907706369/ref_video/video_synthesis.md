### Format A: The "Speed Build" (Efficiency)
1.  **0-3s:** Box on floor -> Snap fingers -> Frame built.
2.  **3-6s:** "Klick" sounds (ASMR) of tubes connecting.
3.  **6-10s:** Putting on the cover (zipping up).

5.  **15s+:** CTA ("Hol es dir jetzt").
*   **Why it works:** Appeals to the German love for efficiency and "Kinderleicht" (child's play) assembly.

### Format B: The "Sceptic" (Trust)
1.  **0-5s:** "I didn't believe a fabric wardrobe could hold my winter coats."
2.  **5-10s:** Loading it with heavy items (weights, water jugs, thick coats).
3.  **10-15s:** Shaking it explicitly to show stability.
4.  **15s+:** "Okay, it's stable. Link here."
*   **Why it works:** Directly addresses the "cheap quality" objection.

## 6. German Market Psychology | å¾·å›½å¸‚åœºå¿ƒç†

**Cultural Triggers (æ–‡åŒ–è§¦å‘å™¨):**
*   **"Ordnung muss sein" (Order must be):** Germans have a deep cultural appreciation for organization and tidiness. A messy room isn't just annoying; it's seen as a character flaw or loss of control.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Always end with a perfectly organized "After" shot.
*   **"Wer billig kauft, kauft zweimal" (Buy cheap, buy twice):** The biggest fear is buying "Chinaschrott" (Chinese junk) that breaks immediately.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Emphasize "Steel Pipes" (Stahlrohre) and "Robust Fabric" (Robuster Stoff) to counter the cheap price point.
*   **Efficiency & Precision:** They hate wasting time. "5 minutes assembly" is a stronger claim than "Beautiful design".
    *   *Creative Choice:* Use a timer overlay counting down during the speed-build.
*   **Privacy:** Germans value privacy. The "Zipper" feature isn't just for dust; it's to *hide* the mess from guests.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Show the action of zipping it up to "hide" the clothes.
*   **Scepticism of Claims:** They don't believe "Life changing". They believe "Holds 20 Jackets".
    *   *Creative Choice:* Be specific with numbers. Don't say "Lots of space", say "Fits 15 pairs of shoes".
