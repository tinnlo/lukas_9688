## 6. German Market Psychology | å¾·å›½å¸‚åœºå¿ƒç†

**Cultural Triggers (æ–‡åŒ–è§¦å‘å™¨):**
*   **"Ordnung muss sein" (Order is essential):** Germans have a deeply ingrained cultural value for orderliness and structure. A messy cabinet isn't just an inconvenience; it's a mild psychological stressor.
    *   *Creative Choice:* The "Before" state must look genuinely stressful/chaotic to trigger the relief of the "After" state.
*   **"Platzangst" in Kitchens (Small Kitchens):** Many German city apartments (Berlin, Munich) have small kitchens. Optimizing vertical space is a necessity, not a luxury.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Use keywords like "Stauraumwunder" (storage space miracle) and "Kleine KÃ¼che" (small kitchen).
*   **Skepticism of "As Seen on TV" Gadgets:** Germans are wary of plasticky, flimsy gimmicks.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Avoid over-hyped acting. Use calm, factual narration. Show the *stability* (shaking the rack to show it doesn't fall).
*   **Value for Money (Preis-Leistung):** They don't mind spending â‚¬20-30 if it solves a problem permanently, but they hate waste.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Emphasize durability and "Buy once, tidy forever."
*   **Renting Culture:** Germany has a high renter population. Permanent fixtures (drilling) are disliked.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Explicitly mention "Ohne Bohren" (No drilling) or "Einfach hinstellen" (Just place it there).

## 13. Risk / Compliance Notes | é£é™©ä¸åˆè§„æé†’

*   **False Claims:** Do not claim it is "Made in Germany" unless it is (likely imported).
*   **Pricing:** Do not mention a specific price in the *video* visuals if it changes frequently in the shop. Use "Im Angebot" (On sale) instead.

*   **Safety:** Do not show children playing with it or chemicals being stored loosely that could look dangerous. Keep it to standard household cleaning supplies.
