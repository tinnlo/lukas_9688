## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY) | 建议

**DO (建议执行):**
1.  **Start with the Problem:** Instead of "It's cute," start with "What if the power goes out?" or "The best gadget for your next camping trip." (从痛点切入，如“停电了怎么办？”)
2.  **Show, Don't Just Tell:** When mentioning the "Mega strong" light, film in a dark room and switch it on to show the contrast. (展示效果，在暗室中演示手电筒亮度。)
3.  **Add Text Overlays:** Add dynamic text for key features (e.g., "NO BATTERIES NEEDED", "SOLAR POWER") to catch scrollers without audio. (添加动态字幕强调核心卖点。)

**DON'T (避免做法):**
1.  **Don't be Passive:** Avoid weak CTAs like "I linked it." Use "Grab yours before the sale ends" or "Perfect for your emergency kit." (避免被动的CTA。)
2.  **Don't Ignore Audio:** Even with a voiceover, adding a subtle underlying track (suspenseful for emergency, or upbeat for camping) helps retention. (不要忽略背景音乐。)

**OPPORTUNITY (机会点):**
1.  **The "Prepper" Angle:** Market this specifically for blackouts/emergencies (Crisis preparation is a strong niche). (生存主义/应急准备角度。)
2.  **ASMR:** The cranking sound and button clicks could be used for an ASMR-style intro. (ASMR风格：利用手摇和按键声音。)
3.  **Gift Guide:** "The perfect gift for your dad/grandpa" (often hard to buy for). (礼品指南角度。)
