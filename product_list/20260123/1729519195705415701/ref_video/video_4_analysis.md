- **Effectiveness | 有效性:** **Medium**. While the pain point is real, the execution appears standard. It relies heavily on the voiceover to do the heavy lifting rather than a shocking visual "pattern interrupt."

## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY) | 建议与机会

**DO (建议):**
1.  **Show Real Usage (展示真实使用):** Replace stock photos with a simple clip of a hand clicking the battery into a real Makita drill. The "Click" sound is a huge trust signal.
2.  **Add Visual Proof (增加视觉证据):** Show a side-by-side time-lapse of this battery vs. a standard battery running a fan or light until the standard one dies.
3.  **Localize Audio (本地化音频):** Use a more gritty, masculine voice (even if AI) that sounds like a construction worker, not a news anchor.

**DON'T (不要):**
1.  **Rely on Renders (依赖渲染图):** Don't use 100% static images. TikTok is a video platform; motion is required to hold attention.
2.  **Ignore Safety Fears (忽视安全顾虑):** People fear cheap lithium batteries catching fire. Don't ignore this; show a "Safety Chip" or "Overheat Protection" visual to reassure them.
3.  **Overload Text (文字过多):** Don't put the entire transcript on screen. Keep text overlays to 2-3 key words per scene (e.g., "8000mAh", "Hält 3x länger").

**OPPORTUNITY (机会):**
1.  **ASMR Approach (ASMR 风格):** A video focused purely on the sounds: Sliding the battery on, the drill spinning up, the power indicator lighting up. No music, just raw tool sounds.
2.  **"Day in the Life" (一日生活):** "I used this one battery for an entire 8-hour shift without charging." A narrative approach works better than a spec sheet.
3.  **German "Sparfuchs" Angle (省钱角度):** Explicitly compare the price: "Original battery €80 vs. This Battery €XX". Germans love a calculated bargain.
