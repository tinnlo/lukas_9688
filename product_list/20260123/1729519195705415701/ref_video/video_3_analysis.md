## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY) | 建议 (做/不做/机会)

**DO:**
1. **Fix the Pronunciation:** Use a better AI voice (like ElevenLabs) or a human voiceover that correctly pronounces "Makita" and "TÜV".
2. **Show Real Proof:** Instead of CGI chips, show a continuous shot of the battery drilling a hole alongside an original battery to prove the capacity claim.
3. **Show the Label:** If claiming TÜV certification, zoom in on the actual TÜV mark on the physical product label to build trust.

**DON'T:**
1. **Exaggerate Cycles:** 4000 cycles is technically improbable for standard Li-ion cells (usually 500-1000). Savvy German buyers know this is a lie. Stick to "1000+ cycles".
2. **Use Bad Audio:** Never publish an ad where the primary keyword (competitor brand) is mispronounced.

**OPPORTUNITY:**
1. **Seasonal Pivot (Winter):** The "-20°C" angle is strong. Create a video specifically focusing *only* on this: Put the battery in a home freezer for 2 hours, then take it out and immediately start a circular saw. This is a powerful "Torture Test" creative that builds immense trust.
2. **Comparison Video:** "Original vs. Ours" - acknowledge the original is great, but yours is 90% as good for 50% of the price. Honesty sells better in Germany than fake superiority.
