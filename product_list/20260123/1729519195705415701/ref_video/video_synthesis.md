## 2. Creative Formats & Structures | 创意结构与模板

### A. The "Torture Test" (Hard Proof)
1.  **Hook:** Extreme situation (Freezer, Drop test, Continuous drilling).
2.  **Action:** Show the battery surviving/performing.
3.  **Specs:** Overlay "8.0Ah" & "BMS Chip".
4.  **CTA:** "Get the beast for half price."

### B. The "Smart Saver" (Logical Appeal)
1.  **Hook:** Price comparison graphic.
2.  **Logic:** "Same cells, same fit, half the price."
3.  **Proof:** Show it powering the same tool as the original.
4.  **CTA:** "Invest the savings in better drill bits."

### C. The "Endurance Runner" (Time-lapse)
1.  **Hook:** "Standard Battery vs. MAWIRON".
2.  **Action:** Split screen time-lapse of two lights/fans.
3.  **Climax:** Left side dies, Right side keeps going.
4.  **Result:** "2x Runtime confirmed."

## 3. Proof & Trust Mechanics | 证据与信任机制

*   **Visual Compatibility:** You MUST show the battery sliding into a **real** Makita tool. The "fit" is the #1 fear for third-party accessories.
*   **The "Weight" Test:** Show the battery on a scale. Heavier usually equals more cells/capacity. Germans understand this proxy for quality.
*   **TÜV/CE Marks:** Don't just say it. Zoom in on the physical label on the battery showing these marks.
*   **Native Audio:** **CRITICAL.** The current videos failed because of robotic TTS mispronouncing "Makita". Use a deep, masculine, native German voice (Dialect: Standard German or slight working-class accent).

## 6. German Market Psychology | 德国市场心理 (MANDATORY)

To win in Germany, you must pivot from "Hype" to "Reliability".

| Cultural Trigger | Production Choice | Why? |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **"Wer billig kauft, kauft zweimal"** (Who buys cheap, buys twice) | Avoid "Crazy Discount" aesthetics. Use "Smart Investment" framing. | Germans fear cheap goods break instantly. Position as "High Value", not just "Low Price". |
| **Data over Emotion** | Show the multimeter reading / Show the stopwatch. | Germans trust measurement. "8.0Ah" means nothing without proof. "3 hours runtime" is better. |
| **"Alles hat seine Ordnung"** (Orderliness) | Show the battery clicking *perfectly* into place. | Fit and finish suggest internal quality. A wobbly battery is a rejected battery. |
| **Skepticism of "China Gadgets"** | Highlight "German Warehouse" or "TÜV Specs" or "CE Mark". | Counteract the fear of unsafe, exploding imports. |
| **The "Sparfuchs" (Savvy Saver)** | Explicitly calculate the ROI. | "2 batteries for the price of 1 original" appeals to financial prudence, not just greed. |

## 7. Visual Editing Playbook | 视觉剪辑打法

*   **No Static Slideshows:** The previous videos failed because they were boring slideshows. Use video footage for at least 80% of the clip.
*   **Overlay Discipline:** Do not cover the tool with text. Put text in the "safe zones" (top center or bottom above description).
*   **Color Grading:** Desaturate slightly and increase contrast to look "Industrial". Avoid the oversaturated "Toy" look.
*   **Speed:** fast cuts (0.8s - 1.5s) are good, but ONLY if the audio matches.

## 8. CTA Patterns & Timing | CTA 节奏与时机

*   **Visual:** Large bold arrow pointing to the bottom left anchor.
*   **Audio:** "Click the link" must happen *while* the arrow appears.
*   **Timing:**
    *   **0-20s:** Value/Problem.
    *   **20-25s:** Offer (Price).
    *   **25-30s:** Hard CTA ("Bestelle jetzt").

## 12. Risk / Compliance Notes | 风险与合规提醒

*   **Trademark:** NEVER say "Makita Battery". Say "Battery **compatible with** Makita". In German: "**Kompatibel mit** Makita". This is legally crucial.
*   **Safety Claims:** Do not claim "Fireproof" unless certified. Stick to "Overheat Protection" (Überhitzungsschutz).
*   **Capacity:** Ensure 8.0Ah is technically defensible (weight/cell count). If it's actually 4.0Ah labeled as 8.0Ah, you risk returns and bans. If unsure, focus on "High Capacity" without emphasizing the number too hard.
