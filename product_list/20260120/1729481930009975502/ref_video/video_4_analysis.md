## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY)

**DO:**
1.  **Add Text Overlays:** Add text like "Dein Katze hasst ihren Napf?" (Your cat hates her bowl?) to reinforce the hook visually for sound-off viewers.
2.  **Stronger CTA:** End with a clear text overlay: "Jetzt bestellen & 50% sparen" (Order now & save 50%) or "Link in Bio".
3.  **Improve Lighting:** Ensure the "After" shot looks brighter and more inviting than the "Before" shot to subliminally signal "better."

**DON'T:**
1.  **Rely on Subtlety:** The "strain" in the first 3 seconds must be obvious. Don't use a shot where the cat looks normal; find a clip where the discomfort is visible.
2.  **Leave the Ending Hanging:** The video ends at 10s without a firm directive. Don't let the loop restart without telling the user what to do.

**OPPORTUNITY:**
1.  **ASMR Angle:** Record the satisfying sound of the cat crunching happily in the new bowl (Ceramic sounds nice).
2.  **Vet Endorsement Angle:** Use a voiceover that sounds more authoritative: "Tierärzte empfehlen erhöhte Näpfe..." (Vets recommend raised bowls...).
3.  **Humor:** "My cat eats better than I do" angle to relate to the pampering owner demographic.
