## 4. Selling Points & Proof | 卖点与证据
*   **"Fire" Internet (Speed):** The primary hook is speed improvement.
*   **Dead Zone Elimination:** Specifically mentions placing it in a "second room" to extend range.
*   **Easy Installation:** Implicitly shown by just plugging it into the wall.
*   **Proof Moment:** The creator claims personal usage ("Used for 3 months") – a verbal proof of reliability.

## 5. Trust Signals | 信任信号
*   **Personal Vouch:** "I have used this for 3 months." (Long-term test claim).
*   **Physical Demo:** Seeing the actual device plugged in makes it tangible.
*   **Direct Address:** The "Hey bro" tone builds a peer-to-peer recommendation vibe rather than a corporate ad.

## 6. CTA & Offer | 号召与优惠
*   **Framing:** "Link below video, orange basket."
*   **Urgency:** None explicitly stated in script, but the short format implies "get it now."
*   **Offer:** Relying on the low price point typical of these items (implied by "TikTok Shop" context).

## 7. Replication for German Market | 德国市场复制指南
*   **Tone:** Shift from "Hey bro" to a slightly more practical but enthusiastic tone ("Endlich überall WLAN" - Finally WiFi everywhere).
*   **Visuals:**
    *   **CRITICAL:** Ensure the plug shown fits German sockets (Type F/Schuko or Type C). If the video shows a US/UK plug, do not reuse the footage directly without a disclaimer or localized cutaway.
    *   **Speed Test:** Germans love data. Show a `Speedtest.net` app screen going from 5Mbps (Red) to 100Mbps (Green).
*   **Compliance:** Avoid "Guaranteed speed" claims. Use "bis zu" (up to) or "verbessert das Signal" (improves signal).
