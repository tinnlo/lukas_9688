**Format A: The "Neighborly Advice" (UGC Review)**
*   **Concept:** "I found this and had to share it."
*   **Structure:**
    1.  **Hook:** "Stop buying X" or "Look at this."
    2.  **Demo:** Clearing a messy path (Before/After).

    4.  **CTA:** "Link is here."

**Format B: The "Torture Test" (Performance)**
*   **Concept:** Proving the specs through extreme action.
*   **Structure:**
    1.  **Hook:** One-finger lift (Weight test).
    2.  **Challenge:** Blowing wet, heavy leaves (Power test).
    3.  **Endurance:** "Still running on battery 1..." (Battery test).
    4.  **CTA:** "Get the deal."

## 6. German Market Psychology | å¾·å›½å¸‚åœºå¿ƒç† (MANDATORY)

**Cultural Behaviors & Preferences:**
1.  **"Ordnung muss sein" (Order must be):** Germans have a deep psychological need for tidiness, especially in semi-public spaces like the "Vorgarten" (front yard) and sidewalk.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Focus on the *edges* and *corners*. Show the blower achieving perfect precision in hard-to-reach spots.
2.  **Skepticism of "Gadgets":** Germans often view cheap tools as "Spielzeug" (toys) that will break instantly.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Use the "Makita Compatibility" as a proxy for industrial quality. If it takes a pro battery, it feels like a pro tool.
3.  **Value != Cheap:** They don't just want the lowest price; they want "Preis-Leistungs-VerhÃ¤ltnis" (Price-Performance Ratio).
    *   *Creative Choice:* Explicitly do the math. "A branded blower costs â‚¬120. This costs â‚¬33 and includes 2 batteries. That is smart savings."
4.  **The "Feierabend" (End of work day) Respect:** They value their free time.
    *   *Creative Choice:* Frame the product not as a "better tool" but as a "time saver". "Done in 5 minutes so you can enjoy your beer/coffee."
5.  **Senior Independence:** A rapidly aging population wants to maintain their homes without help.
    *   *Creative Choice:* The "One Finger" lift isn't just a feature; it's a promise of independence for older users.
