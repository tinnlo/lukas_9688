## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY)

**DO:**
1.  **Target Language Minorities:** If marketing in Germany, consider Turkish or Polish versions. This video proves niche language targeting works.
2.  **Show the "Yuck" Factor:** Don't just show clean surfaces; show the dirt *leaving* the surface (the transition).
3.  **Include a Safety Tip:** Replicate the "Don't do X" segment. It disarms skepticism.

**DON'T:**
1.  **Don't Fake the Dirt:** Use real accumulated dust/grease. Fake dirt (like chocolate sauce) looks like an ad; real grime looks like a review.
2.  **Don't Over-Edit:** Keep the cuts fast, but don't use flashy transitions that distract from the cleaning action.
3.  **Don't Ignore Audio:** Even if visual, the narration here drives the "pain point" narrative. Ensure the script focuses on *feelings* (relief, satisfaction), not just specs.

**OPPORTUNITY:**
1.  **ASMR Version:** A version with NO music, just the sound of the steam hissing and the "whoosh" of cleaning could work well for the "Satisfying" niche.
2.  **Cost Comparison:** Add a line about how much money is saved by not buying chemical cleaners anymore (ROI argument).
3.  **Gift Angle:** "The perfect gift for mom/wife" (Risky, but works for practical audiences).
