## Scripts

The "Health Check Narrative" format focusing on the *meaning* of data, not just numbers. Uses vulnerability (showing "bad" metrics) to build trust with German audiences who are skeptical of too-good-to-be-true claims. Perfect for New Year fitness resolution targeting.

### Structure (38s)
- Hook: "I thought I was healthy" skepticism (0-4s)
- Conflict: Revealing red warning metric (4-12s)
- Realization: Understanding hidden body composition (12-24s)
- Solution: Daily tracking empowerment (24-33s)
- CTA: Call to action for control (33-38s)

### Visual Strategy
| Seconds | Visual | Purpose |
|:--------|:-------|:--------|
| 00-04 | Creator in gym clothes looking confident, flexing arm | Hook: Relatable false confidence setup |
| 04-08 | Stepping on scale, weight shows normal "65 kg" | Subversion: Normal weight ≠ healthy |
| 08-12 | Phone screen reveal: RED bar on "Body Fat 28%" | Conflict: Visual shock of hidden problem |
| 12-16 | Close-up of concerned face looking at phone | Emotion: Vulnerability builds trust |
| 16-20 | App showing muscle mass bar (lower than expected) | Education: Weight doesn't tell whole story |
| 20-24 | Scrolling through water percentage, BMI, visceral fat | Depth: Multiple health indicators |
| 24-28 | Finger tapping "History" tab showing 7-day graph trend | Solution: Tracking enables improvement |
| 28-33 | Sitting on floor with kettlebells, looking motivated with phone | Lifestyle: Fitness context integration |
| 33-38 | Holding scale + phone together, determined expression, pointing to link | Empowerment: Take control CTA |
