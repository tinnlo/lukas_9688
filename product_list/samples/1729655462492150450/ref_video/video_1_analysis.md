## 12. Replication Insights

- **Production Budget:** **Low.** Smartphone + Product.
- **Equipment Needed:** Phone, optional lapel mic for clear audio.
- **Key Success Factor:** **The "Skeptic-to-Believer" Arc.** Don't just show the product; show someone *doubting* it and then being won over.
- **Reproducible Elements:**
    1.  Start with a partner calling the other.
    2.  Partner refuses/complains.
    3.  Force try the product.
    4.  Genuine reaction of relief.
    5.  End with a joke/unrelated promise (like the cat) to keep it light.

## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY)

**DO:**
1.  **Leverage Skepticism:** Start ads with "I thought this was a scam" or "I didn't want this" to align with skeptical viewers.
2.  **Include "B-Plots":** The "Cat" story makes the ad feel like entertainment. Keep adding small narrative layers.
3.  **Zoom on Mechanism:** Ensure the deep-kneading action is shown close-up when the "Deep Tissue" claim is made.

**DON'T:**
1.  **Don't be too polished:** If the lighting is too perfect, the "Schatz, komm mal" opening feels staged and fake. Keep it raw.
2.  **Don't rush the reveal:** Let the partner's annoyance linger for a second to build contrast for the relief later.
