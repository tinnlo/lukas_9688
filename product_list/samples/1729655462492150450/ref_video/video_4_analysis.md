## 13. Recommendations (DO/DON'T/OPPORTUNITY)

**DO:**
1.  **Show the Pain:** Start with a relatable problem (e.g., rubbing a sore neck) *before* showing the product.
2.  **Add Social Proof Overlays:** If it's the "most popular," show a screenshot of 5-star reviews or sales numbers as an overlay.
3.  **Use a Human Model:** Show the device *on* a person's neck to demonstrate fit and relaxation, rather than just holding it.

**DON'T:**
1.  **Rely solely on "Popularity" claims:** Users are skeptical. "Show, don't just tell."
2.  **Ignore the Sound:** Add a satisfying ASMR sound of the motor or relaxing background music to enhance the "massage" mood.
3.  **Rush the CTA:** Give a specific reason to click *now* (e.g., "Flash Sale ends soon").

**OPPORTUNITY:**
1.  **ASMR/Sensory Angle:** Focus purely on the sound and visual of the "squishy" massage heads for a "Satisfying" video tag.
2.  **Gift Angle:** "The perfect gift for your stressed partner" – pivots the audience from self-use to gifters.
3.  **Comparison:** "Old Massager vs. This Bionic Hand" – clear visual upgrade comparison.
