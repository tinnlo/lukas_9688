# ElevenLabs v3 (alpha) — VO Grammar & Prompting Practice (30–40s Shorts)

Goal: make German TikTok VO sound **human, emotional, and non-robotic** (irregular pacing, varied phrasing, natural emphasis) when using **ElevenLabs v3 (alpha)**.

This is a practical writing guide for the text you feed into ElevenLabs (not a linguistics primer).

---

## What v3 (alpha) is great at (and what to avoid)

v3 (alpha) is optimized for **emotionally rich, expressive delivery** and performance.

It usually sounds best when the input:
- Reads like **spoken language**, not copywriting
- Has **clear beats** (line breaks) and **clean punctuation**
- Uses **selective** acting cues (not “cued every line”)

Avoid:
- Over-directing every line (too many cues can feel synthetic)
- Perfectly even pacing (sounds like narration, not TikTok)
- Feature dumps without “human glue” (reactions, asides, stakes)

---

## Minimal cue philosophy (v3 works better with less)

Use cues to set *intent*, then let the model perform:
- 0–2 cues in the hook
- 1–3 cues in the middle
- 1 cue in the CTA

If it already sounds good: remove cues first before rewriting text.

---

## Punctuation & line breaks (the real “grammar”)

### Line breaks = beat changes

One idea per line. Use short “micro-lines” to force natural breaths:
- “Ganz kurz.”
- “Pass auf.”
- “Und jetzt kommt’s.”

### Periods create confidence

German VO becomes more human when you use more periods than in writing:
- “Das ist keine Spielerei.”
- “Das funktioniert.”

### Dashes and ellipses (sparingly) for “thinking out loud”

Use 1–3 per script:
- `—` for pivots/interruptions: “Ich dachte erst—ne.”
- `…` for suspense/soft landing: “Und das Beste ist…”

---

## Irregular pauses (without sounding staged)

Preferred ways to create “human timing”:
1) **Micro-lines** (best)
2) **Self-correction** (“Also—nicht das Case. Die Earbuds.”)
3) **Punctuation** (`…` / `—`)
4) **Explicit pause** `[pause 150ms]` only when a beat must land

Rule: 0–2 explicit pause cues per 30–40s script.

---

## Cue vocabulary (safe, reusable)

Keep cues single-purpose and at the start of the line:
- Intensity: `[soft]` `[neutral]` `[bright]` `[firm]`
- Emotion: `[warm]` `[curious]` `[amused]` `[reflective]` `[skeptical]`
- Delivery: `[understated]` `[matter-of-fact]` `[whisper]`

Notes:
- `[matter-of-fact]` = plain, neutral “info delivery”
- If a line feels over-acted: switch to `[understated]` or remove the cue.

---

## “Anti‑AI flavor” checklist (fast QA)

- Sentence lengths vary (short hits + medium sentences)
- At least 2 human beats (reaction, aside, or self-correction)
- No repeated cadence (don’t start every line with “Und…”)
- Feature list has rhythm (mini-lines, not one long enumeration)
- CTA is short and confident (no over-selling)

## Practical timing guide (30–40s)

Very rough pacing (varies by voice and style):
- 30s: ~65–90 words
- 35s: ~80–105 words
- 40s: ~90–115 words

If it sounds rushed:
- cut one feature, add periods, remove clauses

If it sounds flat:
- add 1 reaction line + 1 micro-line + 1 “clou” beat

---

## Rewrite moves to make it more emotional (pick 2–3)

1) Add a tiny stake: “Wenn du viel reist…”
2) Add contrast: “Sieht nach Spielzeug aus—ist aber clever.”
3) Add micro reaction: “Ich hab’s nicht erwartet, aber…”
4) Add a human moment: “Im Café, wenn du kurz mithören willst…”
5) Add a crisp closer: “Link ist unten. Kurz anschauen.”
